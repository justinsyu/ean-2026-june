Vor Bio

Participation Direct abstract-affiliation evidence. The local dataset is the abstract supplement (European Journal of Neurology, Volume 33, Supplement 1), which does not include an exhibitor or sponsor listing, so participation is characterized from author affiliations only. One abstract, EPV-1005, carries a Vor Bio author affiliation (Boston, MA); it is a trial-design update for the company's global Phase 3 telitacicept study in generalized myasthenia gravis (gMG). Vor Bio is the one entity in this single-company brief with a confirmed author-affiliation abstract, and this section preserves that direct-evidence framing while treating telitacicept product mentions elsewhere as portfolio alignment, not authorship.

Portfolio & strategy relevance Vor Bio (formerly Vor Biopharma; Nasdaq: VOR; Cambridge and Boston, Massachusetts) restructured in May 2025, winding down its engineered hematopoietic stem cell and CAR-T oncology work in acute myeloid leukemia (trem-cel/VOR33 and VCAR33, both terminated), reducing headcount by approximately 95%, appointing Jean-Paul Kress as chief executive, and pivoting to autoimmune disease supported by a $175 million private placement (BioPharma Dive). On 25 June 2025 the company entered an exclusive license with RemeGen for telitacicept covering global rights excluding Greater China, with an initial payment of $125 million ($45 million upfront plus $80 million in warrants) and potential milestones exceeding $4 billion plus tiered royalties (Vor Bio release, SEC Form 8-K). Telitacicept is a recombinant fully human TACI-Fc fusion protein that neutralizes both BLyS (also called BAFF) and APRIL, blocking B-cell maturation and plasma-cell differentiation and reducing autoantibody production (Shi et al., 2023, Dhillon, 2021); the gMG dose studied is 240 mg subcutaneously weekly. In China, telitacicept is approved by the NMPA for systemic lupus erythematosus (conditional, March 2021), rheumatoid arthritis, generalized myasthenia gravis (AChR-antibody-positive, 27 May 2025; RemeGen), IgA nephropathy (conditional, 8 June 2026; GlobeNewswire), and Sjogren's disease (8 June 2026); it is investigational in the United States and European Union (NMPA/China). Vor Bio's lead global indication is generalized myasthenia gravis, and it positions telitacicept's dual BAFF/APRIL (upstream B-cell) mechanism as distinct from the FcRn antagonists and complement inhibitors that otherwise define the gMG market (ClinicalTrialVanguard).

Strongest EAN abstract signals
  • EPV-1005direct, Vor Bio affiliationTrial-design update for a global Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled study of telitacicept in generalized myasthenia gravis (gMG); the abstract carries a Vor Bio author affiliation (Boston, MA). The registered protocol (NCT06456580, "UPSTREAM MG") targets 180 participants, with change from baseline in MG Activities of Daily Living (MG-ADL) at Week 24 as the primary endpoint and Quantitative Myasthenia Gravis (QMG) score at Week 24 among key secondary endpoints. The abstract reports design only; no efficacy data are attributed to it.
  • EPO-0310portfolio-aligned (telitacicept, RemeGen Phase 2)Post hoc domain-specific analysis of the RemeGen Phase 2 gMG trial (NCT04302103; weekly subcutaneous telitacicept 160 mg, n=14, or 240 mg, n=15). Both doses produced improvements across all QMG subdomains from Week 4; the 240 mg dose showed a significantly greater improvement in ocular scores at Weeks 8 and 12 (p=0.037), and participants with a history of thymoma (n=14) showed a numerically greater total QMG reduction than those without (n=15). The authors are the RemeGen trial investigators, not Vor Bio; this is product-mentioned portfolio alignment, not company-authored evidence.
  • EPO-0637mechanism landscape (BAFF)Mechanistic study in AChR-antibody-positive gMG reporting that long-term eculizumab reduces peripheral CD19+ B cells, memory B cells, and antibody-secreting cells by inhibiting monocyte C5a-C5aR1-mediated BAFF secretion. The finding implicates BAFF (B-lymphocyte stimulator) in B-cell survival in MG, the cytokine telitacicept neutralizes together with APRIL, and is mechanistic landscape context rather than telitacicept evidence.
  • EPO-0640B-cell comparatorMINT (NCT04524273), a Phase 3 placebo-controlled trial of inebilizumab (anti-CD19 B-cell-depleting antibody) in gMG, frames B cells as having "a key upstream mechanistic role" in gMG pathogenesis through autoantibody production. Adverse events of special interest were infrequent (inebilizumab 10.1%, n=12; placebo 16.0%, n=19). Landscape evidence for upstream B-cell-directed mechanisms, not a telitacicept comparison.
  • EPO-1054unmet needCross-sectional real-world survey of 917 gMG participants across six countries (United States, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, United Kingdom; January 2024 to November 2025). Among participants on treatment, 40.4% were prescribed corticosteroids (43.1% at a dosage of 20 mg/day or more) and 37.2% biologics, with biologics administered a mean of 2.3 times per month. The authors note that reducing steroid burden and frequent biologic administration while maintaining efficacy could benefit this population.
  • EPO-0301comparator (FcRn vs C5)Multicentre retrospective study of 93 AChR-positive thymoma-associated MG participants comparing anti-C5 complement inhibition (n=58) with anti-FcRn cycles (n=35) over 12 months. Anti-FcRn "short responders" (mean cycle interval under 8 weeks; 43% of the FcRn cohort) had significantly inferior outcomes; anti-C5 showed greater mean MG-ADL improvement (-6.43 versus -3.21) and corticosteroid reduction (-10.8 versus -6.6 mg/day). Competitive-intelligence context on FcRn redosing variability and on thymoma-associated disease.
  • EPO-0311comparator (FcRn)Pooled subgroup analysis of repeated rozanolixizumab (anti-FcRn) treatment cycles across MycarinG and its open-label extensions; 129 participants received at least two symptom-driven cycles, with up to 13 cycles administered upon symptom worsening and mean MG-ADL change at Day 43 ranging from -3.2 to -6.0 across cycles. Illustrates the cyclical, symptom-driven redosing pattern characteristic of the FcRn class.
  • EPO-0309real-world burdenFrench national-claims (RELIEF) study of 859 gMG participants diagnosed in 2022. Within one year, 72.1% experienced an exacerbation and 10.4% a myasthenic crisis; among MG participants with a crisis, 81.6% required mechanical ventilation. Real-world evidence on early disease instability that motivates early, sustained disease control.
Current public activity reviewed (sourced)
  • UPSTREAM MG global Phase 3, NCT06456580 (ClinicalTrials.gov) (accessed 2026-06-28): Vor Biopharma-sponsored Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled study of subcutaneous telitacicept versus placebo in gMG, with change from baseline in MG-ADL at Week 24 as the primary endpoint, an estimated 180 participants, recruiting across 14 countries, started 17 July 2024, estimated primary completion May 2027; Vor Bio states initial results are expected in the first half of 2027.
  • China Phase 3 gMG efficacy, RemeGen (PR Newswire) (accessed 2026-06-28): in the completed China Phase 3 (registry NCT05737160, telitacicept 240 mg weekly versus placebo), 98.1% of telitacicept participants achieved a 3-point or greater MG-ADL improvement at Week 24 versus 12.0% on placebo (presented at the American Academy of Neurology meeting, 9 April 2025); Vor Bio cites an approximately 4.8-point placebo-adjusted MG-ADL improvement at Week 24. A peer-reviewed primary-results publication for this Phase 3 was not located in PubMed as of 2026-06-28.
  • 48-week China Phase 3 open-label extension data (BioSpace; AANEM, 29 October 2025) (accessed 2026-06-28): continuous-treatment participants reached a mean MG-ADL change of -7.5 and a mean QMG change of -9.8 at Week 48, with safety described as comparable to placebo and consistent with prior studies.
  • Phase 2 gMG results, Yin et al., Eur J Neurol 2024 (PubMed 38726639) (accessed 2026-06-28): the published RemeGen Phase 2 (n=29; the dataset underlying EPO-0310) reported mean QMG reductions from baseline to Week 24 of 7.7 (160 mg) and 9.6 (240 mg); the primary publication reports pooled outcomes and does not present a registry-confirmed thymoma subgroup.
  • Mechanism review, Shi et al. 2023 (PMC10277628) and Dhillon, Drugs 2021 (PubMed 34463932) (accessed 2026-06-28): describe the TACI-Fc structure, dual BLyS/APRIL neutralization, and the first China SLE approval (March 2021) at 160 mg subcutaneously weekly.
  • NMPA IgA nephropathy approval, 8 June 2026 (GlobeNewswire) and Sjogren's disease approval (GlobeNewswire) (accessed 2026-06-28): document the broadening China autoimmune label (NMPA/China), portfolio context rather than gMG evidence.
  • REMESLE-1 SLE Phase 3, NCT05306574 (terminated) and Sjogren's Phase 3, NCT07404865 (recruiting) (accessed 2026-06-28): the broader Vor Biopharma telitacicept program; the legacy oncology assets (NCT04849910 VOR33, NCT05984199 VCAR33) are terminated, consistent with the 2025 pivot.
  • Independent steroid-sparing real-world study, Fang et al., Front Immunol 2025 (PubMed 40196119) and independent thymoma-associated MG study, Zhu et al. 2026 (PubMed 41483096) (accessed 2026-06-28): single-center Chinese studies, not Vor Bio activity; cited to mark where independent literature, rather than Vor Bio evidence, covers steroid-sparing and thymoma questions.

Competitor flag (not a systematic competitor scan; flags are illustrative only): the obvious comparators for telitacicept in gMG are the FcRn antagonists (efgartigimod, rozanolixizumab, nipocalimab) and the complement inhibitors (eculizumab, ravulizumab, zilucoplan, gefurulimab, cemdisiran), with B-cell-directed agents (inebilizumab, rituximab, CD19 CAR-T) as the closest mechanistic neighbours to telitacicept's upstream B-cell approach; EAN 2026 abstracts EPO-0301, EPO-0311, EPO-0640, and EPO-0637 illustrate these classes.
