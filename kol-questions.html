Conference-Derived Questions

Topic Relevant Abstracts KOL Questions
MS biomarkers OPR-021 separated biomarker roles: NfL predicted next-year relapse, while GFAP predicted PIRA. EPO-0364, EPO-0466, and EPO-0673 added nuance around timing, progression, and GFAP versus NfL. In stable MS with normal sNfL but persistently rising age-adjusted GFAP, how do you decide whether to intensify surveillance, obtain advanced MRI/OCT/cognition testing, or consider a therapy change?
Anti-CD20 MS risk EPO-0371 suggested durable ocrelizumab disease control, while EPO-0368, EPO-0137, OPR-118, and OPR-121 raised monitoring, discontinuation, and infection-risk questions. How do you approach older stable MS patients on anti-CD20 therapy who have recurrent respiratory infections and falling IgG, including lab follow-up, vaccine-response testing, dosing changes, immunology referral, IVIG, or switch/de-escalation discussions?
Natalizumab JCV/PML EPO-0996 found discordant PML risk classes depending on anti-JCV assay. EPO-0929 suggested host biology may also influence anti-JCV positivity. For a patient with JCV index 0.9-1.5 after more than 2 years of natalizumab and historically highly active MS, how do you weigh q6-week extended-interval dosing versus switching, and what MRI or host-risk factors shift your comfort level?
Seronegative vs false-positive neuroimmunology EPO-0664 supported antibody-negative autoimmune encephalitis as a real clinical entity, while EPO-0814 showed a high false-positive burden for serum-only CASPR2 results. EPO-0815 added mimic risk. In discordant cases, how do you think through low-titer serum antibody results, confirmatory testing, and objective clinical/MRI/CSF evidence when deciding whether to treat as seronegative AE/MOGAD/NMOSD?
gMG goals OPR-085 showed patient-acceptable symptom state is not captured fully by low MG-ADL. EPO-0152, EPO-0146, and EPO-0308 separated symptom response, sustained outcomes, steroid burden, and early exacerbation risk. For patients with MG-ADL 0-2, how do you balance sustained MSE/MM, PASS, steroid dose, rescue/exacerbation history, and adverse-effect burden when deciding whether current control is good enough?
CIDP/immune neuropathy EPO-0052 highlighted POEMS misdiagnosed as CIDP. EPO-0053 described acute-onset CIDP. EPO-0211 and EPO-0219 pointed to nodopathy and poor IVIG response. In antibody-negative possible CIDP with atypical features or weak objective IVIG response, how do you structure reassessment before stopping CIDP-directed treatment or relabeling as nodopathy, mimic, or CIDP variant?
EVT boundaries OPR-127 supported IVT+EVT in isolated M2 occlusion, while EPO-0677 found similar outcomes but higher sICH for broader distal-to-medium vessel occlusions. EPO-0521 raised ultra-late EVT uncertainty. After the guideline algorithm, are there scenarios where you still transfer or treat medium-vessel occlusion outside dominant M2 0-6h, and how do vessel location, deficit, core/penumbra, collaterals, and access risk shape that discussion?
Tenecteplase late/remote stroke EPO-0188 showed workflow gains in a remote region. EPO-0193 suggested late-window tenecteplase benefit in selected settings. EPO-0517 framed the efficacy-safety tradeoff versus alteplase. For perfusion-selected 4.5-24h ICA/M1/M2 LVO at a non-EVT hospital, how do transfer timing, imaging confidence, BP control, monitoring capacity, and rescue protocols influence whether you use tenecteplase before transfer?
Breakthrough stroke on DOAC OPR-102 and OPR-123 emphasized competing mechanisms and correctable contributors after breakthrough AF stroke. OPR-124 found continuation versus switching did not clearly separate outcomes. In verified therapeutic DOAC-breakthrough AF stroke with no clear competing mechanism, how do you weigh continuing the same DOAC, switching, LAAO, or adding a mechanism-specific antiplatelet strategy?
Anti-amyloid AD treatment OPR-076 showed real-world amyloid lowering with lecanemab/donanemab. EPO-0166 raised regional residual amyloid dynamics. EPO-0007 raised ARIA monitoring uncertainty. In post-clearance anti-amyloid patients with global subthreshold amyloid PET but focal cortical retention, how do you reconcile visual and Centiloid/regional findings, and how do PET, tau, fluid biomarkers, MRI, and clinical course shape stop, maintain, repeat-PET, or restart discussions?
Biological AD diagnosis EPO-0499 showed reclassification under 2024 biological criteria, including a non-AD/prion concern. EPO-0493 focused on p-tau217 gray-zone classification. EPO-0326 showed biomarker thresholds vary by pathology burden. In atypical or mixed-pathology cases with gray-zone p-tau217, how do you sequence amyloid PET, CSF ratios, tau PET, and clinical follow-up before treating AD as the actionable diagnosis?
Migraine response EPO-0088 showed MIDAS/HIT-6 are not interchangeable with headache diaries. EPO-0409 and EPO-0410 showed non-pain and prodromal symptoms can persist despite headache improvement. For patients below monthly migraine-day response thresholds, how do you weigh HIT-6/MIDAS, PGIC, acute-medication days, and patient-selected non-headache symptoms when deciding whether preventive therapy is still worthwhile?
