TG Therapeutics

Participation One ePoster, the BRILL real-world study (EPV-1275), lists an author affiliation to TG Therapeutics, Morrisville, USA, alongside Neuraxpharm Pharmaceuticals (Barcelona and Langenfeld), the European marketing authorization holder for Briumvi; this is direct author-affiliation evidence. Portfolio & strategy relevance TG Therapeutics' lead product is BRIUMVI (ublituximab-xiiy), a glycoengineered anti-CD20 monoclonal antibody engineered for enhanced Fc gamma receptor IIIa binding and antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity (TG pipeline; Steinman et al., NEJM 2022, PMID 36001711). It is approved for relapsing forms of multiple sclerosis (RMS): the FDA indication covers clinically isolated syndrome, relapsing-remitting disease, and active secondary progressive disease in adults (FDA, US), and the EMA indication covers adults with RMS with active disease defined by clinical or imaging features (EMA, EU). The approved regimen is a 150 mg first infusion over approximately 4 hours, a 450 mg infusion two weeks later over approximately 1 hour, and 450 mg every 24 weeks thereafter over approximately 1 hour (DailyMed label, FDA, US). Approvals followed the phase 3 ULTIMATE I and II trials versus teriflunomide (annualized relapse rate 0.08 versus 0.19 and 0.09 versus 0.18; Steinman et al., NEJM 2022): FDA on 2022-12-28 (US) and EMA marketing authorization on 2023-05-31 with Neuraxpharm as the holder (EMA, EU). The current ublituximab development effort includes a subcutaneous formulation (positive phase 1 reported 2026-06-03; phase 3 NCT07211633 enrolled), a consolidated intravenous dosing study (ENHANCE, NCT05877963), a planned pediatric RMS trial (NCT07220252), and real-world, pregnancy, and lactation registries; progressive MS is being addressed by a separate modality, the allogeneic CD19 CAR-T candidate azer-cel, rather than ublituximab (2026 milestone guidance).

  • EPO-0783landscape, mechanism, no TG affiliationIn 101 participants receiving ocrelizumab, the FCGR3A (CD16) V158F low-affinity genotype was associated with earlier B-cell repopulation when dosing intervals were extended (odds ratio 2.17, p = 0.04), and ocrelizumab binding to natural killer cells was lower in FF than VV donors (p < 0.001), supporting a CD16-specific, Fc gamma receptor-dependent effect on anti-CD20 activity.
  • EPO-0337landscape, anti-CD20 classTime-and-motion analysis of intravenous-to-subcutaneous ocrelizumab conversion in a high-volume infusion center reduced total active clinical time from 320 to 60 minutes (a reduction of 81%), releasing approximately 65 clinical hours and 50 chair-hours per month at 15 subcutaneous administrations per month.
  • EPO-0665landscape, anti-CD20 classReal-world intravenous-to-subcutaneous ocrelizumab transition (51 participants completing patient-reported measures): 82.3% were satisfied or very satisfied with administration and 81.8% perceived time savings versus infusion, with CD19-positive B cells maintained at 5 cells/µL or fewer in 96% and no observed relapses.
  • OPR-121landscape, anti-CD20 classDanish nationwide registry (11,140 participants, 24,403 treatment episodes, 2017 to 2024): anti-CD20 agents carried the highest infection risk among disease-modifying therapies relative to teriflunomide (hazard ratio 3.0, 95% CI 2.7 to 3.5), underscoring infection surveillance as a class-level management priority.
  • OPR-118landscape, anti-CD20 classProspective two-center cohort: in clinically stable participants, ocrelizumab discontinuation was not associated with a significantly increased risk of combined inflammatory activity (hazard ratio 0.91) over a median 28.5 months of follow-up, with an apparent plateau of anti-inflammatory benefit after approximately 29 to 30 months of treatment and rising reactivation risk beyond 30 to 32 months off-treatment.
  • OPR-021landscape, biomarkerIn two large observational cohorts (2,329 participants), elevated serum neurofilament light chain predicted relapse within one year (odds ratio 2.00, 95% CI 1.63 to 2.45), and a yearly reduction in glial fibrillary acidic protein during B-cell-depleting therapy was associated with a 67% lower risk of progression independent of relapse activity (hazard ratio 0.33, 95% CI 0.18 to 0.61).
Current public activity reviewed (sourced)
  • BRIUMVI US prescribing information (DailyMed) (accessed 2026-06-28): FDA-approved (US) for relapsing forms of MS; dosing is 150 mg first infusion over approximately 4 hours, 450 mg at week 2 over approximately 1 hour, then 450 mg every 24 weeks over approximately 1 hour, with required premedication; label carries class warnings for infections and reduced immunoglobulins.
  • EMA EPAR Briumvi (accessed 2026-06-28): EU marketing authorization granted 2023-05-31 for adults with relapsing MS with active disease; marketing authorization holder Neuraxpharm Pharmaceuticals S.L. (EMA, EU), the same Neuraxpharm entity co-listed on the BRILL ePoster.
  • ULTIMATE I/II pivotal trials, Steinman et al., NEJM 2022 (PMID 36001711) (accessed 2026-06-28): two phase 3 trials (549 and 545 participants) reporting lower annualized relapse rates with ublituximab than teriflunomide, with no significant difference in 12-week confirmed disability worsening; registrations NCT03277261 and NCT03277248.
  • Five-year open-label extension, Cree et al., JAMA Neurol 2026 (accessed 2026-06-28): continued ublituximab was associated with lower 24-week confirmed disability progression than delayed initiation (8.0% versus 14.3%; extension NCT04130997).
  • ENHANCE (NCT05877963) (accessed 2026-06-28): TG-sponsored phase 3b evaluating a consolidated intravenous regimen, including a single-arm part for participants with suboptimal experience on a prior anti-CD20; treatment-satisfaction (TSQM-9) is a secondary, not primary, measure.
  • Subcutaneous phase 3 (NCT07211633) and subcutaneous phase 2 (NCT07503873) (accessed 2026-06-28): non-inferiority and device studies of subcutaneous ublituximab; positive phase 1 results were reported 2026-06-03, with pivotal topline expected year-end 2026 to first quarter 2027.
  • Pediatric RMS trial (NCT07220252) (accessed 2026-06-28): planned phase 2/3 in participants aged 10 to under 18, with a non-inferiority comparison versus fingolimod.
  • ENABLE real-world registry (NCT06433752), BRIUMVI Pregnancy Registry (NCT06433765), and PROVIDE breast-milk study (NCT06143514) (accessed 2026-06-28): TG-sponsored observational studies; ENABLE (target 2,000 participants) has no results posted, with primary completion projected for 2032.
  • BRIUMVI HCP access resources and BRIUMVI financial assistance (accessed 2026-06-28): US patient-support and commercial copay offerings, including a Quick Start provision; these are US, commercial-payer programs (FDA, US). No equivalent EU or UK patient-support or copay program was identified on the company sites reviewed (the patient pages returned HTTP 403 to automated retrieval and were corroborated through indexed results).
  • No publicly visible TG-sponsored ublituximab trial in primary progressive MS was identified (searched ClinicalTrials.gov by sponsor "TG Therapeutics" and by intervention "ublituximab" with condition "multiple sclerosis", 2026-06-28; the active TG MS trials list primary progressive MS as an exclusion criterion); this does not exclude nonpublic or planned activity.
  • No publicly visible TG-sponsored ublituximab head-to-head trial versus another anti-CD20 agent, and no TG ublituximab MS trial with neurofilament light chain or a patient-reported outcome as a primary endpoint, were identified (searched ClinicalTrials.gov sponsor and intervention sweeps and PubMed, 2026-06-28; patient-reported and biomarker measures appear only as secondary endpoints); thin results may reflect limited public material rather than confirmed absence.
Gap-anchored insights
  • SignalSubcutaneous anti-CD20 conversion releases substantial infusion-unit capacity (active clinical time reduced 81%, from 320 to 60 minutes; EPO-0337) and yields high real-world satisfaction and perceived time savings (82.3% satisfied; EPO-0665).
    Current positionBRIUMVI is intravenous, with a one-hour maintenance infusion after the starting dose (DailyMed, FDA/US); a subcutaneous formulation has positive phase 1 data and an enrolled phase 3 (NCT07211633), with topline expected year-end 2026 to first quarter 2027.
    Gap evidenceNo publicly visible peer-reviewed time-and-motion or chair-capacity analysis quantifies the one-hour BRIUMVI infusion against competitor intravenous anti-CD20 regimens, and subcutaneous ublituximab efficacy data are not yet reported (searched ClinicalTrials.gov TG sponsor and ublituximab intervention sweeps and PubMed "ublituximab subcutaneous infusion time capacity", 2026-06-28); this does not exclude nonpublic analyses.
    Recommendationmarket access / value evidence Quantify the chair-time and capacity dividend of the existing one-hour infusion now, and prespecify operational and treatment-satisfaction endpoints in the subcutaneous program, using EPO-0337 and EPO-0665 as the methodological rationale; these are class-level abstracts, so the rationale is hypothesis-generating rather than a BRIUMVI efficacy claim.
