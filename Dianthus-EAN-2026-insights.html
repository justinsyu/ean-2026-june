Direct abstract-affiliation participant: five EAN 2026 abstracts carry Dianthus Therapeutics author affiliations

Dianthus Therapeutics

Participation Direct abstract-affiliation participant. Authors listing a Dianthus Therapeutics affiliation (New York) appear on five EAN 2026 abstracts: three reporting the gMG MaGic trial (EPO-0148, EPO-0477, EPO-0639), one Phase 3 CIDP trial-design poster (EPV-1007), and one mechanistic poster (EPV-1048). Dianthus is also named in the disclosure statements of approximately 23 further abstracts, reflecting investigator consulting or honoraria relationships rather than company authorship. Sponsor, exhibitor, and satellite-symposium status at EAN 2026 was not confirmed in publicly accessible EAN congress materials as of 2026-06-28; this section is anchored to the confirmed author affiliations.

Portfolio & strategy relevance Dianthus Therapeutics is a clinical-stage company whose lead asset is claseprubart (formerly DNTH103), described by the company as "an investigational, long-acting monoclonal antibody engineered to potently and selectively inhibit the active form of C1s, a clinically validated target in the classical complement pathway" (Dianthus pipeline page). The company states claseprubart is "engineered with YTE half-life extension technology" and "intended to be the first subcutaneous complement therapy that can be self-administered as infrequently as once every four weeks" (Dianthus pipeline page). The disclosed clinical pipeline spans three neuromuscular indications: generalized myasthenia gravis (gMG; MaGic Phase 2, NCT06282159, and EMERGE Phase 3, NCT07647510), CIDP (CAPTIVATE Phase 3, NCT06858579), and multifocal motor neuropathy (MMN; MOMENTUM Phase 2, NCT06537999). The classical complement pathway is mechanistically implicated across these acetylcholine-receptor-antibody and complement-mediated peripheral nerve disorders, which is the basis for treating the EAN abstract signals as portfolio-relevant.

Direct abstract match Five abstracts carry an author affiliation to Dianthus Therapeutics, New York. EPO-0477, EPO-0148, and EPO-0639 report the gMG MaGic Phase 2 trial (NCT06282159) and constitute direct, Dianthus-affiliated clinical evidence. EPV-1007 (CAPTIVATE Phase 3 CIDP trial design) and EPV-1048 (active-C1s mechanistic positioning) are direct, Dianthus-affiliated records that appear in the supplement as title, authorship, and affiliations only, with no structured abstract body or results. Abstracts reporting other active-C1s, anti-C2, or anti-C1q agents (for example riliprubart, empasiprubart, and tanruprubart) carry no Dianthus affiliation and are treated as landscape comparator evidence; a shared complement mechanism indicates class relevance, not company attribution.

Strongest EAN abstract signals
  • EPO-0477directMaGic, a global Phase 2 randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial in acetylcholine-receptor-antibody-positive (AChR+) gMG (65 participants randomized 1:1:1 to claseprubart 300 mg, claseprubart 600 mg, or placebo subcutaneously every 2 weeks for 13 weeks). At Week 13, MG-ADL improved by 4.6 points (300 mg; p = 0.0113) and 5.4 (600 mg; p = 0.0006) versus 2.8 for placebo; QMG improved by 4.4 (300 mg; p = 0.0144) and 4.5 (600 mg; p = 0.0111) versus 2.0; MG Composite improved by 8.7 and 8.6 versus 3.1. No serious adverse events, no serious bacterial infections, and no autoimmune activation were reported, and significant MG-ADL improvement appeared as early as Week 1.
  • EPO-0148directMultiple-responder analysis from MaGic (claseprubart 300 mg). 63% of claseprubart participants achieved a dual response, defined as MG-ADL improvement of at least 3 points and QMG improvement of at least 4 points, at Week 13 versus 14% for placebo (odds ratio 29.00; p = 0.0006). Dual responses were observed as early as Week 1 (median time to response Week 3), and all Week-13 dual responders maintained response for at least 6 weeks. p-values are one-sided with nominal significance at alpha 0.1.
  • EPO-0639directMinimal symptom expression (MSE) analysis from MaGic (claseprubart 300 mg). At Week 13, 37% of claseprubart participants achieved MG-ADL MSE (MG-ADL of 1 or less) versus 14% for placebo (odds ratio 3.81; p = 0.0550); 43% achieved MSE at least once during the study versus 14% (odds ratio 4.72; p = 0.0231). MSE was observed as early as Week 1, and all participants achieving MSE sustained it for at least 6 weeks. p-values are one-sided with nominal significance at alpha 0.1.
  • EPV-1007direct · designCAPTIVATE: a pivotal Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial design for claseprubart in CIDP, co-authored by Dianthus Therapeutics with international peripheral-nerve investigators. This virtual ePoster record appears in the supplement as title, authorship, and affiliations only; no methods or results are reported. The registered trial is NCT06858579.
  • EPV-1048direct · mechanismMechanistic positioning poster on the potential advantages of active-C1s (upstream) versus downstream complement blockade in myasthenia gravis, co-authored by Dianthus Therapeutics. This virtual ePoster record appears in the supplement as title, authorship, and affiliations only; no underlying data are reported.
  • EPO-0054landscape · competitorRiliprubart (Sanofi), a first-in-class humanized IgG4 monoclonal antibody that also selectively inhibits activated C1s, in an open-label Phase 2 CIDP trial (NCT04658472). At Week 76, quality of life (EQ-VAS), pain (EQ-5D-5L), and fatigue (Rasch-built Fatigue Severity Scale) improved across standard-of-care-treated, refractory, and naive groups. Same mechanism (active C1s) and same indication (CIDP) as the Dianthus CAPTIVATE program; this is comparator evidence, not Dianthus evidence.
  • EPO-0877landscape · competitorEmpasiprubart (argenx), a monoclonal antibody that binds C2 and blocks the classical and lectin pathways while sparing the alternative pathway, in the Phase 2 ARDA study in MMN (NCT05225675; 54 IVIg-dependent participants). Patient-reported outcomes improved versus placebo, including Patient Global Impression of Change improvement of 94% versus 11% (cohort 1) and 83% versus 44% (cohort 2). Same indication (MMN) as the Dianthus MOMENTUM program, with an adjacent complement mechanism; this is comparator evidence, not Dianthus evidence.
  • EPO-0723landscape · class safetyTanruprubart (Annexon), a monoclonal antibody inhibiting the upstream classical-complement component C1q, in the Phase 3 GBS-02 trial in Guillain-Barre syndrome (safety set 241 participants). Encapsulated-bacteria infection rates were similar across treatment and placebo groups, and 95% of participants did not receive prophylactic antibiotics. This illustrates class-level infection-safety evidence for upstream classical-pathway inhibition; Guillain-Barre syndrome is not a Dianthus indication, and this is comparator evidence, not Dianthus evidence.

  • EMERGE gMG Phase 3, NCT07647510 (ClinicalTrials.gov) (accessed 2026-06-28): status not yet recruiting; planned enrollment 195; primary endpoint is change from baseline to Week 17 in MG-ADL (an efficacy endpoint, in contrast to the safety primary of the Phase 2 MaGic trial); start listed for 2026-07.
